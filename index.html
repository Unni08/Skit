Forest Protection Movements

India has a total forest cover area of more than 7 lakh sq. Km, which is about 21.71% of the country’s geographical area, as of February 2023. But over the last 3 years, 87 projects have been granted environmental clearance and 2.3 million trees have been marked to be cut down. If this continues, we would be a ‘Breathless’ country in a few decades.
Many people have taken to protecting trees and there have been several movements for the same. Telling about them all will take quite some time. So, here, we would like to enact a few of them, which have earned a name for a safer life.

1. Chipko Movement

{Characters: 3 villagers, Sunderlal Bahuguna, 2 contractors, 1 as a tree}
(In 1970s, there was a plan to remove forests illegally in Uttarkhand, then part of UP. The movement was the result of a spontaneous action of villagers. It was led by Sunderlal Bahuguna)
(A villager is standing nearby. A couple of contractors arrive.)
Contractor 1: For our project, this would be the perfect site.
Contractor 2: Yes, if we clear a few hectares of this forest, we can earn lots of money.
* (Villager hears this and...)
Villager 1: Hey! You can’t cut the forests like that. It’s very important to us. Besides, cutting these trees will harm the nature.
Contractor 2: Look here mister, just go and mind your own business. After all, what do you know about all these developmental projects?
* (Exit contractors. Villager 1 goes to others.)
Villager 1: There’s some news. Some contractors are planning to cut down our forests to build a project.
Villager 2: Oh no! We must do something to protect our trees. It is very important to us.
Villager 3: Yes, I agree. We have to stop them. But the question is how. They are big and rich people from the cities.
(Sunderlal Bahuguna suggests an idea.)
Sunderlal Bahuguna: We must protect our trees. Let’s surround to the trees and stop those contractors from cutting our trees.
Villager 2: You’re right, Sunderlalji. Let’s do it
(On the day, the people stood as Sunderlalji said. The contractors couldn’t cut down the trees.)
Contractor 1: If you want to live go away. We won’t hesitate to chop you along with these trees.
Villager 3: Well then, chop us down.
* (The contractors return back angrily.)
Sunderlal Bahuguna: Ecology is the greatest economy.
(Sunderlal Bahuguna accomplished this with not only members of his village, but with others as well. He was also later able to meet the then PM of India, Indira Gandhi, and it was with his influence that there was a 15-year ban of felling of green trees.)

2. Save Silent Valley Movement

{Characters: 2 govt officials, rest as villagers and KSSP members, Suguthakumari}
(The govt sanctioned a hydroelectric project in 1978. It would submerge 8.3 sq. km of untouched evergreen forest in the Silent Valley in Kerala. The members of KSSP started a protest against the project.)
KSSP 1: Do not destroy this forest in the name of development. We won’t allow it.
KSSP 2: Yes. Go back. Leave the forest alone.
Govt official: Please understand. This project will bring electricity to your homes. It will help industries to grow. We can develop a lot.
KSSP 3: We are not opposing the development. We are protecting the forest. It is an undisturbed forest. We will not let it submerge.
Govt official 2: If you don’t go back, we will take action against you all. So better stop this.
(But the protesters were determined to protect the forest. Poet-Activist Suguthakumari also joined the protest in her own way. She wrote a poem named ‘Marathinu Stuthi’ meaning ode to a tree. It became an important part in the movement.)
Suguthakumari:
You give from the bottom of your roots
to the tops of your leafy locks
to nurture us thankless souls
and what do we give you in return
but fire and axe
You forgive
You forgive everything
But wait
Will mother nature forgive?
Or will she burn us to ashes
With her wrathful gaze?
(Later, PM Indira Gandhi had to obey the public. In 1983, the project was called off. In 1985 PM Rajiv Gandhi inaugurated the Silent Valley National Park.)

3. Jungle Bachao Andolan

{Characters: 3 natives, 2 minsters}
(In 1982, the govt decided to replace the natural Sal trees of Singbhum District of Jharkhand. The tribals there protested it, which became the Jungle Bachao Andolan.)
(In the minister’s office…)
Minister 1: This is a project of making plantations of Teak in Jharkhand.
Minister 2: I think it will be a good project. The teakwood is expensive and it has many gains. But, where’s the space for such a huge plantation?
Minister 1: My plan is to replace the Sal in the forests of Jharkhand.
Minister 2: Hmm… okay
(Word spreads out about this plan of the govt. The natives, who are tribals living in the forest, react violently.)
*(During the protest…)
Native 1: Don’t destroy our natural forests. We won’t let you change our forests.
Native 2: Yes. There shall be no plantations here. The Teak will not replace Sal.
Native 3: If you do that, it will be a great harm to the environment.
All together: Plantations go back.
(Finally, they succeeded in stopping the large-scale destruction of the Sal forests. This movements not only stopped felling of trees, but also helped bring out voice the tribals, which had previously been ignored by the govt)
